    Tripindi Shraddha

    Tripindi Shradha Puja is Pind daan. If anyone in the family from the last three generations has passed away in a very young age or old age, then those souls cause problems to us. If, for continuous three years, this ritual is not made to departed souls, then the dead gets angry, so to calm them, this offering is mainly made. This is a very important Ritual in Hindu culture.
    As per the ancient scripture Shradha Kamalkar, it is said that Shradha of ancestors should be performed two times a year. If it is not done for many years, then ancestors remain unhappy, causing various problems to next generations.

    Usually, when a middle or old age human is passed away, people do their Pind Daan, Shardha, and other rituals, but when people at a young age, passed away, then not all the rituals are done correctly. Which further causes their soul to being human bondage and drives issues to us; hence Tripindi Shardha is to be done to set those souls free and attain their way to heaven.
    It is essential to do Shradha every year on their death anniversaries along with familiar Shardha has to be done in the month of Bhadrapad "Pitrupaksh," which is known as "Samvatsarik Shardha" and "Mahalaya Shardha." Suppose either of these Shradha is not performed continuously for three continuous years. In that case, it causes Shardha loop that causes pain and problems to our forefathers' souls in our present lives as predecessor expect Moksha through us, which is known as Pitrudosh. And Tripindi Shardha is one way to get rid of this dosha, as specified in ancient scriptures. The person whose birth chart (Kundali) has this kind of dosha has to perform Tripindi Shradha for his ancestor's Moksha. Either married or unmarried people can do this right. An only a single woman cannot do Tripindi Shardha.

    The karmic debt of the forefathers, Pitra Dosh, is expressed in the horoscope as a planetary sequence. Pitra Dosh is a series of bad luck that exists in people's lives due to the curse imposed on them by their forefathers who have passed away. Pitru dosh can cause a lot of stress in the family and cause a lot of restlessness. It may also occur due to ancestors being overlooked and not being given their due in the form of Shradha, charity, or spiritual advancement.
    People who die and leave their bodies join the realm of the forefathers, known as Pitru Loka. Those who live in the pitru Loka suffer from extreme hunger and thirst. On the other hand, they cannot eat anything on their own and must rely on the offerings made to them during Shraadha rituals. As a result, the children need to reconcile them by continuing to observe the Shraadha ceremony. Failure to do so will provoke the forefathers' wrath and result in Pitru Dosh.
    Pitra Dosh is not a forefather's curse. It is, however, a karmic debt owed to the ancestors, which must be paid by someone with Pitru dosh in their astrology. In simple terms, a Pitru Dosh appears in a person's horoscope when his or her ancestors have committed specific errors, offenses, or sins. As a result, this person repays the Karmic debt by enduring various punishments levied on him for those debts in multiple areas of his life. This dilemma is better explained by astrology. Beneficial planets avoid producing beneficial outcomes as a result of the inflicted planets' adverse effects.

    Amavasya and Ashtami are the best days to perform the Pitru Dosh Niwaran. On Pitru Paksha, some people perform this pooja. Furthermore, this Pooja must be performed on the last day of Pitra Paksha. This pooja should be done based on the person's Kundli and with the aid of an expert. For the Puja, Tripindi Shradh is used. Alongside the Kund, they perform Tripindi Shradh. On this Puja, people worship three Gods. After performing the Kalash Puja, worship Brahma, Vishnu, Shankar, and Gopal Krishna.
    People perform a Pindh in honour of their ancestors who have passed away. From great-grandparents to uncles, aunts, brothers, sisters, mothers and fathers-in-law, and everyone else in the family who is no longer alive. It also includes the names of Gurujis who have died. These Pindhs are worshipped and given Kala Til, Jal, Pushpa, and Tulsi Leaves. The Vidhaan lasts 1.5 to 2 hours.
    In the name of the forefathers who are no longer living, Bhakt will offer food and clothing.
